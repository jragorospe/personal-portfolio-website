Humble Beginnings

My family and I immigrated to Canada when I was a toddler. Growing up in Canada has been an incredible privilege, one that has been allotted due to the hard work and sacrifice of my parents. Hard work, gratitude, and a heart for service are values instilled in me from the very start and I carry them in everything I do.

Some of my favourite past times have been playing games. I still have vivid memories of every time I received a console, the craziest of which was when my godfather unexpectedly handed me a Nintendo 64 after church. It was an incredible console but my favourites were the PSP and Nintendo GameCube. Playing Patapon 2 and MGS: Peace Walker on road trips to the States and countless hours of Super Smash Bros. Melee cemented my love for video games.

Growing up, it never occurred to me that I could be the one to make experiences like these for others. But in hindsight, my dream of being an inventor and my love for music, the world of game development was a perfect match for my analytical and artistic brain. It's a realization that has been suppressed by the need for practicality but it's never to late to start the pursuit.

Overcoming Hardship

University was a time for exploration and growth. I pursued a Computer Science degree at the University of Calgary and a minor of Philosophy. I have always had a knack for solving problems and people-problems are definitely the most interesting to me, especially in relation to ethics.

Despite my interest in school, my University experience was not that great. At the start, a lot of things were happening in my life. My parents were going through a rough patch in their marriage, and my girlfriend was diagnosed with Stage 4 melanoma. This caused my grades to plummet as I burried myself in work instead of acknowledging my anxiety.

I found myself in academic probation twice, failing to meet the requirements both times. Having my parents find the letter to withdraw from the University and writing the appeal, forced me to acknowledge that these issues were effecting me more than I was willing to admit. I sought help from mentors and friends and was able to overcome my anxiety issues. My girlfriend beat cancer, we got married, my parents sorted out their marriage, and I was able to complete school in 5 years, going from a 1.0 GPA to 3.4 GPA.

I'd like my story to be a reminder that you can overcome what seems impossible at the time with the help of others. Whenever I'm reminded of this time of my life I remember Arnold Schwarzenegger's address to the students at the University of Houston.

Moving Forward

During University, I had the oppourtunity to work at Shokunin during the height of its popularity with the launch of Netflix's, The Final Table. Our chef was one of the finalist on the show, which garnered him celebrity status and an influx of new clientele. I've had the privelege to work with world class chefs, interact with high profile clients, and work with the most talented team.

I am eternally grateful for Shokunin. It was an incredibly impactful oppourtunity, teaching me important teamwork and management skills, how to work in a high stress environment, and was pivotal for helping me move out and paying for my University.

Now I am pursuing my passions as a game developer, hoping to make an impact through the experiences I create. I am priveleged to have been given the oppourtunity to work in the game industry in Calgary, a city where the industry is virtually non-existent. I'm currently at Zugalu, helping to create Thrive, a medieval city builder inspired by the likes of Anno and Total War. I continue to stay diligent in my learning and hope to make my mark on the industry some day.
